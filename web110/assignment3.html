The next activity I had completed was about reviewing the "honeycomb model" and reviewing popular social media sites to see which sites implemented the honeycomb model well and which didn't. I had learned what the honeycomb model is and its components. I knew that social media sites had to follow some sort-of model because most social media sites are very similar.
For example, Facebook implements a few of the components of the honeycomb model very well. Facebook fosters coversations, it has many different groups you can join, many of the users relate to each other on many levels, and users reveal a lot of their daily lives. However, Facebook doesn't have the best reputation due to selling data. It also falls short on the presence component because it can be hard to see if someone is online and available.
The other site I had looked at was Reddit. Reddit implements the honeycomb model in a pretty lacking way. For example, Reddit has a ton of communities, a lot of people relate to each other, it is all about having conversations, and it allows people to share posts easily. However, users typically don't reveal much about themselves due to a lot of bad people using Reddit, the reputation of Reddit is horrible, especially online, and there is almost no way of knowing when someone is online and available to talk to. I can use this in my life when building websites, especially social media based websites, to make sure that the website or social media aspect of the website will be a success. I can also use this to talk to clients and give them my own input on how certain aspects that they may have wanted coded will impact user's use of the website.
